Aerospace Students Fly to America
 
 
A group of 19 final year aerospace students toured Aerospace facilities in Washington DC and Indiana between 15-21‌ May‌ 2016‌. They had back stage passes to see the work of NTSB, NASA Goddard and Purdue University, plus a little free time to explore the Smithsonian Institute, the main attractions of Washington DC‌ and a final half day in Chicago‌.
 
 
One Aerospace Systems Engineering student noted:

Visiting the NTSB opened my eyes to the multitude of engineering disciplines required to successfully determine the most probable cause of an air accident. Many of these disciplines such as avionics and propulsion were covered in my degree meaning that I was able to understand the technical content of the tour. The trip also consisted of a visit to the NASA Goddard Space Flight Centre and Purdue University, both of which were fantastic networking opportunities and exposed me to the diverse career prospects within the US aerospace industry.
 
 
 
 
Collaborative Project with Emirates Aviation University
 
As part of a new partnership deal, undergraduates from Coventry University studying Aerospace courses were involved in a collaborative project with Emirates Aviation University. Students were given the opportunity to visit the Emirates Maintenance Centre and explore Dubai; a city well known for its luxury shopping malls and modern architecture. Dubai is also home to the World’s tallest building to date – The Burj Khalifa tower (828m).
 
 
One Aerospace Systems Engineering student said:

I was delighted to be given an opportunity to visit Dubai, Emirates Aviation University, Emirates Aviation and Maintenance Centre by Coventry University. The trip gave me a chance to experience the Middle Eastern culture, the modern architecture of Dubai, the climate, the amazing food, and a chance to interact with other multi-ethnic university students studying in Dubai.

Ultimate Student Survival Tips
 
The idea of starting university and meeting new people can be daunting for some, but do not worry – we have come up with the top survival tips to make sure you’re ready for university life.

• Get involved and make friends.
Many of you will be spending much of your university life with your new classmates. You will be working together on group projects for 3 to 4 years so it’s important to become friends.
 
• Prepare your equipment the night before lectures.
Double check that you have lecture note paper, books, or any other equipment before class starts so you can fully participate in each session.
 
• Plan your outfits for your first week in advance.
Thankfully, university is not a fashion show but if you plan ahead, it is one less thing to think about. Comfortable shoes are a must as you’ll be walking a lot.
 
• Don’t leave study until the last minute.
We have all made this mistake, but you need balance if you are to thrive and enjoy university life. Stay on top of your work as much as possible to get the best results.
 
• Finally, get ready to have the best time.
Your university life only comes around once, and you will always remember your time as a fresher. Be responsible, but don’t take it too seriously and enjoy yourself. First year really is the best!
